Plant a Tree!

Which Trees To Plant In Los Osos?

These trees are recommended for the planting in Los Osos because they are either natives or friendly to the Central Coast, require little water, grow in diverse soil conditions and climates of Los Osos, and enhance the beauty of the community.

Burgundy Willow Myrtle - Agonis flexuosa - Thrives in Los Osos. Good garden tree. Birds love it. Paperbark Tree - Melaleuca quinquenervia Enchanting bark & showy flowers.

Channel Island Oak - Quercus tomentella - Large, great tree. Attracts birds, mammals, butterflies. Coast Live Oak - Quercus agrifolia - Native to Los Osos. Attracts birds, mammals, butterflies.

Cork Oak - Quercus suber - Beautiful bark. Attracts birds & squirrels.

Marina Madrone - Arbutus marina - Popular tree, grows well. Beautiful red bark & berries. Monterey Cypress - Hesperocyparis macrocarp - Statuesque CA native with fragrant leaves. Birds love it. Red Flowering Gum - Corymbia ficifolia - Beautiful blooms. Attracts birds & bees.

SC Island Ironwood -Lyonothamnus floribundus - Good garden tree. Grows fast. CA native.

Stone Pine - Pinus pinea - Wonderful tree with beautiful canopy.

Torrey Pine - Pinus torreyana - Large tree. CA native.

Wilson Fruitless Olive - Oleo europaea - Narrow gray foliage. Good in gardens. Bird favorite.

NOTE: In San Luis Obispo County, you must obtain a permit to remove a tree, and you are required to plant a replacement. Contact Planning & Building: 805-788-2076


Tree Planting Tips

Keeping trees watered is important during the first year. Keep the soil and mulch moist but not soggy. In dry weather, water generously every 7-10 days. Avoid watering so much that you see standing water.

Install a rainwater capture system to use for irrigation. Consider a location that won't block a neighbor's view. Do not use fertilizer, potting soil or chemicals, as these can kill young trees.
